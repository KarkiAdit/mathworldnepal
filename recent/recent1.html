MWN's commitment for the International Youth Math Challenge

Aayush Acharya
on November 22, 2021

Have you ever come across a thought that you had that one opportunity to show your mathematical ability? If yes, International Youth Math Challenge (IYMC) has given you the perfect platform to fulfill these inner aspirations. With over ten thousand math enthusiasts from almost hundred countries, IYMC engages you in a fierce, yet friendly, competition worldwide. Anyone strictly under 19 years of age can participate in this competition which has three rounds: Qualification round, Pre-final round, and the Final round. Learn more about IYMC from the official website of the organizing committee: https://iymc.info/.

MWN wants to help students prepare for this event. To make this possible, our Content team is working on the Lets Talk Mathematics project. Under this project, content creators of MWN are making contents, which are closely related to the syllabus that IYMC tries to cover. Not only will these contents help learners understand the topics better, but also make them confident in solving various math questions. For achieving this, MWN has been creating quality interactive lectures and video solutions. Similarly, the informative articles and the mesmerizing blogs are sure to enhance our learners’ chances in the final rounds because they are profoundly researched and cover present day Math topics which IYMC focuses on. All these resources are readily available in our website and YouTube channel, which you can access in one button-click. Furthermore, we are always willing to expand our team to enhance the quality of content and ensure the smoother flow of topics to the math enthusiasts.

When it comes to understanding the competition, the best place to get the primary information would probably be its website. However, if you are still stumbled upon something or have some unanswered questions, you can always contact us in our Discord server https://discord.com/invite/4kADpwN3P9. Two members from the MWN family have been serving as the ambassadors for IYMC from Nepal, and they are more than ready to personally mentor you throughout your participation. Additionally, we will also help you prepare for solving those questions from the IYMC set that require research. Our intellectual team, which contains bright math heads from all over Nepal, teachers who are spread over 21 districts in Nepal, and university students who are spread across the globe will be handy in helping us achieve this.

While it has almost been a year since the formation, the MWN team has taken quite significant leaps to help the students. For instance, we have been in close contact with the Olympiad participants to ensure that they guide students through webinars hosted live on Facebook Page of MWN. In the days forward, we aim at collaborating with math professionals to further strengthen the bridge between math enthusiasts and experts. Apart from our regular duty of creating good content, we have been helping students, who have contacted us, either by solving their questions ourselves or by guiding them towards the resources of the questions.

In addition, most of the members of MWN are either high school students or high school graduates, who greatly excel in math. The common vision of all the members of MWN is to guide students systematically—by providing relevant information—to approach various mathematics tournaments. Most of the members of Math World Nepal exhibit a great mathematical ability and are willing to assist the next generation of math enthusiasts to not just delegate the country worldwide, but also bring home trophies and recognition. As a youth startup, we feel that this small help from our side could be a small peck to Nepalese students to further explore and delve in the field of STEM.

Most importantly, our goals as a group extends way beyond just making contents that prepare enthusiasts for various tournaments. We acknowledge that the core of mathematics is way beyond solving a few questions, and more focused towards the real-world application. As a result, through our networking with math experts, we have assured guidance in our research projects. Our research primarily focuses on identifying various factors in environment that might affect a certain change. Keeping Mathematical reasoning as fulcrum of our researches, we aim to tackle real-world problems by conducting a proper sample survey and doing a profound data analysis.

Moreover, despite being focused on mathematics fundamentally, we plan on incorporating students who exhibit great computer/programming skills to bring even better presentations and math animations to our topics. All in all, our group aims at incorporating mathematics with other various fields to create our unique trademark—one that is unparallel to any limitations and delves deep into scientific knowledge for creating a World of Math.
